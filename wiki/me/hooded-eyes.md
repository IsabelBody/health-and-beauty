# Hooded eyes — reference (yours + technique)

Confirmed Jun 2026. You have **hooded + deep set + almond**, wide-set, neutral tilt (not up- or downturned), prominent brow bone. This note is the canonical eye-shape makeup guide; [[face-blueprint]] links here for placement.

## What “hooded” means

Skin from the **brow bone** folds forward over the mobile eyelid. When you look straight ahead, little or no lid shows and the **natural crease is hidden or partly hidden** (partial vs full hood).

| Type | What you see with eyes open |
|------|-----------------------------|
| **Partial hood** | Some crease/lid still visible |
| **Full hood** | Crease fully covered; lid reads as one plane |

Most people are born with hooded eyes; hooding can also increase with age as skin laxity changes. Hooded is **not the same as droopy** (ptosis = low lid margin / weak lift muscle); hooded is extra fold skin covering the crease.

**Self-check (matches you):** With eyes relaxed and open, if crease shadow disappears under the upper lid skin, you’re hooded. Makeup on the “classic crease” often vanishes when you open your eyes; liner may **transfer** onto the skin below the brow.

## How this combines with your other eye traits

| Trait | Interaction with hooded |
|-------|-------------------------|
| **Deep set** | Socket is recessed; dark shadow *in* the natural crease can make eyes look smaller. Place depth **below the brow bone** (where bone creates a natural shadow), not in the low crease. |
| **Almond + neutral tilt** | Elongated shape suits **outer emphasis** and soft lifted wing; avoid rounded, downward liner. |
| **Prominent brow bone** | Hood sits under a strong brow shelf — shadow placement and brow grooming both affect how “open” the eye reads. |
| **Wide-set** | Inner-corner brightening still works; don’t over-extend liner inward. |
| **Long lashes, outer emphasis** | Aligns with hooded advice: **curl + outer mascara**; uncurled lashes read downturned on you. |

Your own description (“deep lidded,” crease deep when visible, hood covers lid when open) fits **partial hood on a deep-set almond eye**.

## Golden rules (apply every eye look)

1. **Eyes open, relaxed** — mirror at eye level; no pulling skin or raising brows while applying. What you see open is what others see.
2. **Ignore the low natural crease for depth** — build the “crease” **above** the fold, along the shadow under the brow bone (fake crease / elevated contour).
3. **Blend up and out** — brush strokes toward the **temple**, not horizontal scrubbing in a hidden crease.
4. **Check with eyes open** after each shadow step; lift placement until color reads.
5. **Primer + thin, waterproof products** — hood skin rubs lid makeup; primer, thin liner, setting powder on transfer zones reduce smudge.
6. **Keep mobile lid lighter** — matte or soft satin on lid; depth on brow-bone shadow zone; shimmer **center lid or inner corner**, not across the hood.

## Eyeshadow map (hooded + deep set)

```
[Brow bone — highlight optional, very soft]
[Contour zone — matte transition + depth: BELOW brow bone, above hidden crease]
[Mobile lid — lighter / controlled satin or soft shimmer]
[Lash line — tightline or thin liner]
```

| Zone | Do | Avoid |
|------|-----|--------|
| **Contour / “crease”** | Matte cool taupe, plum, berry (deep winter); outer V; blend toward temple | Heavy dark through entire natural crease only |
| **Lid** | Light neutral or soft satin; optional shimmer **center lid** | Dark matte all over lid; glitter on hood |
| **Outer corner** | Depth at outer third + outer socket (your dump: outer corner, outer socket) | Black block on whole lid |
| **Lower lash** | Soft smoke outer lower third if desired; keep subtle for daily | Heavy lower ring that drags eye down |
| **Inner corner** | Light matte or controlled shimmer (wide-set friendly) | — |

**Cut crease (optional):** Concealer or light shade on mobile lid, cut line **above** natural fold — opens the eye; half cut crease (line stops mid-lid) is easier for everyday.

## Eyeliner

| Technique | Notes for you |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Thin upper lash line** | Hugs lashes; maximizes visible lid |
| **Outer third only** | Full lid line often disappears under hood except inner/outer tips |
| **Tightline upper waterline** | Defines without eating lid space |
| **Mini wing / soft lifted wing** | Draw with eye **open**; angle wing toward temple, not straight out; “bat wing” / steep lift if full wing vanishes |
| **Waterproof pen or gel** | Reduces transfer to hood (you use KVD brown liquid — keep thin) |

**Avoid:** Very thick liner, pulling lid taut, rounded droopy shapes, heavy downward extension (matches your placement blueprint).

## Mascara & lashes

- **Curl every time** — your lashes point down uncured and read downturned.
- **Focus:** upper lashes, **outer upper** emphasis (already in [[../../areas/makeup/routine-daily]]).
- **Lower lash:** minimal for daily so eye stays lifted.
- **Goal:** lift + separation; lengthening formulas work well on hooded eyes.

## Brows

Hooded eyes read more open when brows are **groomed and slightly lifted** (arch or tail elevation), not heavy underneath. You don’t fill — maintain wax shape; avoid lowering or thickening the underside of the brow, which shortens lid space.

## Longevity checklist

- Eyeshadow primer (or thin concealer + set) on lids  
- Thin layers; set liner if needed with matching shadow  
- Light powder **only** where liner transfers to hood  
- Finish one eye before the other if symmetry is hard (common on hooded lids)

## Fits your existing standards

- **Face priority:** brows + eyes first — hooded technique supports that, doesn’t change lip strategy ([[../learnings/matte-lips-anchor-eyes]]).  
- **Color:** cool deep winter socket shades; outer emphasis over apple-style eye looks.  
- **Concealer:** under-eye stays **under crease / hollow**, not dragged over lid (your under-eye routine).  
- **Placement doc (processed):** outer corner, outer socket, satin/soft reflective lid, avoid heavy dark through full crease — **aligned** with this research.
