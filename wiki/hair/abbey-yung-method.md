# Abbey Yung method

Reference for the viral **Abbey Yung method** — a flexible hair-care framework, not a fixed 11-product daily routine. Use [[product-matching]] to pick products and [[step-rules-and-conflicts]] before stacking steps.

**Active base routine:** [[../../areas/hair/routine-wash-day]] (Abbey Yung–aligned, from Jun 2026). Prior context: [[../learnings/hair-hydration-primary-issue]].

## What it is

Abbey Yung (certified trichologist; TikTok/YouTube) published her personal routine after others named it “the Abby Young method.” She defines it as:

> A flexible framework that helps you build a routine based on your **scalp type**, **hair type**, and **scalp and hair needs** — not a one-size-fits-all checklist.

- **Full form:** 11 optional step *categories* (pre-wash → between-wash).
- **Minimum form:** shampoo + conditioner or mask + leave-in with heat protection.
- **Starter form (often cited):** clarifying shampoo (occasional) + everyday shampoo (scalp) + conditioner (strand thickness) + one leave-in.

She did **not** intend everyone to run all 11 steps every wash. Steps are added a few times per week as needed.

## Core principles

1. **Scalp is skin** — choose shampoo for scalp behaviour (oily, dry, balanced, medicated if needed).
2. **Strand thickness** (fine / medium / coarse) matters more than curl pattern for conditioner, mask, and leave-in weight.
3. **Hair density** — how much hair you have — affects volume of product, not always formula choice.
4. **Cleanse effectively** — “training” hair to go longer between washes is discouraged; wash when **your** scalp needs it.
5. **Condition every wash** — skipping conditioner is a common breakage/dryness source in her model.
6. **Protect lengths** — leave-in + heat protectant when heat styling.
7. **Change slowly** — one or two new products at a time so you can read feedback (greasy, heavy, dry, itchy).

### Benchmark questions (wash day + days after)

**Scalp:** itchy? flakes? greasy how soon after wash?

**Lengths:** tangled after wash? dry? heavy/waxy?

Use answers to swap products — discomfort is feedback, not failure.

## The 11 steps

| # | Step | Role | Typical frequency |
|---|------|------|-------------------|
| 1 | Pre-shampoo bond repair | Strengthen before wash; dry application | 1–2×/week; ≥10 min |
| 2 | Pre-shampoo oil | Hydration, slip, tangling | When dry/tangled; ≥20 min; can be overnight |
| 3 | Clarifying / **deep-clean** shampoo | Remove buildup; reset scalp | ~weekly; oily scalps may use more often |
| 4 | Regular (non-clarifying) shampoo | Everyday cleanse for scalp type | Every shampoo wash |
| 4b | Medicated shampoo | Flakes, itch (zinc pyrithione, ketoconazole, etc.) | Per need; not everyone |
| 5 | Post-shampoo bond repair (in shower) | Damage from colour/heat | ~weekly; not every wash |
| 6 | Conditioner, mask, and/or gloss | Detangle, smooth, shine, frizz | **Every wash**; leave on minutes |
| 7 | Post-wash bond repair | Leave on ~10 min before other products | Optional weekly |
| 8 | Leave-in + heat protectant | Seal moisture; protect from heat | After wash |
| 9 | Styling products | Volume, hold, etc. | As needed |
| 10 | Style sealers | Serum, oil, lotion on styled hair | As needed |
| 11 | Between-wash care | Dry shampoo, overnight oil/treatment | As needed |

**Note on “clarifying”:** Yung uses clarifying / deep-clean / detox / purifying / chelating (hard water) interchangeably in marketing language. Goal is **thorough buildup removal**, not a specific brand label.

## Simplified routines Abbey describes

### Three products

Shampoo + conditioner or mask + leave-in with heat protection.

### Four products (good entry point)

1. Clarifying/deep-clean — occasional reset  
2. Regular shampoo — scalp type  
3. Conditioner or mask — strand thickness  
4. One leave-in you will actually use  

### Example weekly pattern (her personal cadence — adjust)

She often washes **3×/week** with rotating emphasis:

- **Day A:** Pre bond repair + pre-oil + clarify and/or regular shampoo + 1–2 conditioning products (no in-shower bond repair).  
- **Day B:** Clarify + in-shower bond repair + one conditioner/mask (no pre-oil).  
- **Day C:** Same as Day A.  
- **K18 week:** Substitute K18 on bond-repair day per brand protocol (often after clarify; not mixed with other bond products that wash).

If you wash more or less, add “shampoo + condition only” days or drop a bond day — see her YouTube example routines.

## Product philosophy

- Many **drugstore and prestige** options listed; treat as a menu, not a shopping list.
- Rejects **chemophobia** — silicones and sulphates are not blanket banned; match to scalp/hair response.
- **Affiliate / gifted products** — standard influencer disclosure; still test on your hair.

## Expert criticism (balance)

Trichologist **Hannah Gaboardi** (Marie Claire / Newsweek): individual steps are sound, but the **full 11-step stack is excessive** for many; doubling bond repair, mandatory clarifying for minimal routines, and heavy between-wash care may be unnecessary.

**Takeaway:** Use the framework to **diagnose** (scalp clean + lengths conditioned + protected), not to replicate TikTok hauls.

## Medical boundary

Yung’s disclaimers: not a substitute for dermatology for persistent shedding, scalp disease, or allergic reactions. Medicated shampoos for dandruff/psoriasis — use as directed; GP if symptoms persist.

## How this maps to my priorities (Jun 2026)
