Where do I naturally blush? Contouring versus expanding blush 




Questions 
For my face shape, 
what is the optimal blush placement?
Where do I really need concealer?
Where do I need skin tint? 
What is the optimal contour placement? 
Optimal Lip lining?
Where should highlight go. 



You’re basically trying to build a personal facial blueprint for makeup instead of blindly copying tutorials. That’s the right approach, because makeup works best when it’s adapted to structure, proportions, coloring, and contrast level.

Here are the major categories and the highest-value questions to ask yourself.


---

1. Facial Structure Questions

This determines placement.

Face Shape

For your face shape:

Where should blush go to balance proportions?

Should blush be horizontal, lifted, centered, or draped?

Where should contour go?

Should contour shorten, sharpen, soften, or widen?

Where should highlight go?

What areas should stay matte?


Facial Width & Length

Is my face visually long, short, wide, or narrow?

Do I need vertical balance or horizontal balance?

Should makeup elongate or compact the face?


Cheekbones

Are my cheekbones high, low, sharp, soft, prominent, flat?

Should blush sit above, on, or below cheekbones?

Should contour carve or softly shadow?


Jaw & Chin

Is my jaw angular, tapered, broad, delicate?

Does my chin project, recede, appear short, or long?

Should contour sharpen the jaw or soften it?


Forehead

Is my forehead tall, short, wide, narrow?

Should contour reduce forehead height?

Should blush placement compensate for forehead proportions?



---

2. Eye Analysis Questions

This is one of the most important categories.

Eye Shape

Almond, round, hooded, monolid, downturned, upturned, deep-set, protruding?


You already identified deep-set + almond/upturned, which is very useful.

Eye Spacing

Close-set, balanced, or wide-set?

Should inner corners be brightened?

Should liner extend inward or outward?


Lid Space

How much visible lid space do I have?

Do I need lifted shadow placement?

Should shimmer go on lid or inner corner only?


Crease Structure

Is the crease deep, soft, hidden, low?

Should shadow sit above the natural crease?


Lash Balance

Do my eyes need emphasis on outer lashes, center lashes, or lower lashes?

Should mascara focus on lift, length, or density?


Eyeliner Mapping

What eyeliner angle best suits my eyes?

Thin liner or thick liner?

Tightlining or visible liner?

Should liner connect to lower lashline?



---

3. Eyebrow Questions

Brows completely change facial balance.

Are my brows too straight, too arched, too rounded?

What brow shape balances my face?

Where should the brow tail end?

How thick should brows be?

Should brows be soft, fluffy, defined, or sculpted?

What brow undertone works best with my coloring?



---

4. Complexion Questions

This determines base makeup strategy.

Coverage Mapping

This is extremely important and most people never think about it.

Where do I ACTUALLY need coverage?

Which areas already have good skin?

Which areas only need color correction?

Where should skin still show through?


Usually:

Concealer = targeted correction

Skin tint = evening overall tone

Foundation = more uniform/full coverage


Many people overapply base products everywhere unnecessarily.

Texture Mapping

Where is my skin textured?

Where is it dry?

Where is it oily?

Where do products separate?

Which areas need powder vs glow?


Under-Eye Analysis

Is darkness caused by pigmentation, hollowness, shadowing, or veins?

Peach corrector or not?

Brightening concealer or skin-tone concealer?


Finish

Does matte flatter me?

Does glow flatter me?

Satin?

Soft-focus?


Deep winters often suit:

satin

glassy skin with controlled structure

defined contrast

not overly warm bronzy finishes
